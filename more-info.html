the GAMA survey

This survey is one of the most compehensive projects on galaxy formation and evolution ever carried out. Data is collected froom a number of different facilities around the world and used to create a large, in depth study of modern cosmology. from which we can hope to learn more about a number of topics like Dark Matter, the birth of galaxies and how they change over their lifetime.

The facilities that take the data for the GAMA survey spread the globe. ESO own two ground based telescopes located in Chile called the VISTA and the VLT survey telescope, ESA another European company own the Herschel Space Observatory a space based telescope. Australia has two telescopes, ASKAP and AAT whoch are both ground based. The USA contributes the GALEX space based telescope ran by NASA.

For more information go to Gama Survey.

cosmic microwave background radiation

CMB radiation is the residual electromagnetic radiation from The Big Bang. It was discovered accidentally when Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson in the 1960's when they were mapping out radio signals from the Milky Way and noticed a constant signal from all corners of the sky, initially thinking it was something wrong with their equipment they later realised they were the first people measure what is the largest piece of evidence for the Big Bang theory of the creation of the universe we have. For this they later got the Nobel Prize.

For more information search for Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation.
